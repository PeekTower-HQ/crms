## üö® Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### ‚ùå Don't Do This:

1. **Exaggerate deployment status**
   - Be honest: "production-ready, pre-pilot"
   - Don't claim operational deployments that don't exist

2. **Promise unrealistic timelines**
   - 90 days to deploy is realistic
   - 30 days is not (government approvals take time)

3. **Badmouth competitors**
   - Focus on your strengths
   - Acknowledge others' contributions to the space

4. **Be defensive about questions**
   - Questions = interest, not attacks
   - Answer honestly and thoughtfully

5. **Use too much jargon**
   - "Offline-first" not "service workers with IndexedDB cache"
   - "Background checks via USSD" not "USSD gateway API integration"

### ‚úÖ Do This Instead:

1. **Emphasize technical readiness**
   - 91% complete, 144 tests, 200K words docs
   - Show, don't just tell

2. **Be humble about what you don't know**
   - "We need help with government partnerships"
   - "We're seeking deployment coordination guidance"

3. **Tell stories**
   - The offline-first decision
   - The configuration breakthrough
   - The USSD inclusion imperative

4. **Use concrete numbers**
   - Not "well-tested" ‚Üí "144 test cases"
   - Not "documented" ‚Üí "200,000+ words"
   - Not "fast" ‚Üí "90-day deployment timeline"

5. **Express urgency without desperation**
   - "Criminal justice reform can't wait"
   - "We're ready to deploy now, not in 12 months"
